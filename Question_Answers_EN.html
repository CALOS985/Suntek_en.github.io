There can be many factors causing the battery life to be shorter than expected:

1. Batter: Make sure all the batteries are of the same brand and have the same charge. We recommend testing AA batteries (even new ones) with a multimeter before use, and please make sure they are at least 1.5V. If there is one of eight batteries that are poor, your camera could stop working. 

2. ON Mode: Check whether the switch is turned to ON mode which is in a low power consumption state, and the camera/video mode of the device will only be activated when the infrared sensor detects an animal. 

3. Video Mode or Camera&Video Mode: It requires the camera to stay in action longer, consuming the batteries faster. Also, the longer the video, the faster the batteries will drain. 

4. Interval: This feature allows you to select the time elapsed before the camera can detect it again and take the next photo. If you want to capture multiple photos, you will need to use a shorter interval which means more battery consumption.

5. Sequence: This option tells the camera how many photos it should take for each detection. The higher the sequence, the more pictures the camera will take, which will consume the batteries faster. 

6. Low signal: When the signal is low, the camera will try to establish a connection for a longer period, which will consume more batteries. 

Theoretically, the camera can capture thousands of images or videos before the batteries die.
